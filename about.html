My Waste Reduction Journey

Key milestones and experiences that have shaped my sustainable lifestyle.

2025

Expnading the base!

Successfully completed the publicity website for the Zero Waste Lifestyle to attarct people to join in on the fun and quest to help the environemnt!

2023-2024

Advocacy and Lifestyle Integration

By fifteen, waste reduction became a natural part of my routine. I joined my school's environmental club, helping with campus clean-ups and promoting recycling. I also made an effort to consistently use reusable bags, bring my own containers for leftovers, and be more mindful of packaging when shopping. I'd casually share tips with friends and family, just pointing out simple ways to cut down on waste. This phase was about making small, consistent changes that added up.

2020-2022

Deepening Understanding and Practical Solutions

Secondary school brought a critical lens to environmental issues. At thirteen, a geography project on landfills exposed the global scale of waste challenges, prompting me to seek systemic solutions. I started a compost bin for food scraps, and became conscious of "pre-cycling" – choosing products with minimal packaging, buying in bulk, and repairing items. This period was about trial and error in daily waste reduction.

2017-2019

Curiosity and Conscious Choices Begin

Entering my pre-teen years, my understanding deepened. Around age eleven, a science documentary on ocean plastic pollution deeply impacted me. It shifted my focus from just bins to the planet. I began questioning single-use items like cling film and plastic straws, leading to my first personal challenges: using reusable water bottles, refusing plastic bags, and saying no to straws. These small rebellions felt empowering. I also started noticing food waste but wasn't sure how to address it yet.

2014-2016

The Seeds of Awareness

My earliest waste reduction memories, around age eight, involved household rules: blue for paper/plastic, green for glass, black for other trash. School reinforced "Reduce, Reuse, Recycle." A project collecting plastic bottle caps showed me our daily plastic consumption, teaching me waste separation and that "trash" could have a second life.

Green Hacks that I tried

Simple and effective tips to reduce waste in your daily life.

Bring Your Own Bag

Say no to plastic—carry a reusable tote when shopping.

Refill Bottles

Use a refillable water bottle to cut down single-use plastics.

LED Light Bulbs

Switch to energy-efficient bulbs and lower your carbon footprint.

Reusable Mugs

Skip paper cups—bring your own coffee mug.

Plant at Home

Grow herbs or greens to reduce packaging waste and emissions.

Switch Off

Turn off fans, lights, and devices when not in use.

Bike More

Swap short car trips with cycling or walking.

Recycle Smart

Separate recyclables correctly to avoid contamination.

Bulk Buying

Reduce packaging waste by buying dry goods in bulk.
